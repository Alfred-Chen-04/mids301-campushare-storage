## Final English Draft

Every year, more than 1.1 million international students enrolled at U.S. universities face the same annual crisis: when the academic year ends, they must vacate campus housing with no practical place to store their belongings. Commercial self-storage facilities average over $150 per month, are frequently miles from campus, and require a vehicle to access — making them functionally inaccessible for students without cars or local support networks (IBISWorld, 2024; IIE, 2024). Meanwhile, their fellow students who remain on campus hold thousands of square feet of underutilized closet, room, and basement space. The market for connecting these two groups does not yet exist in any structured, trusted form.

CampusShare Storage is a peer-to-peer (P2P) marketplace that matches students who need short-term storage with students who have unused space — all within a single university campus. Access to the platform is gated by `.edu` email verification, ensuring that both Hosts (space providers) and Renters (storage seekers) are authenticated members of the same campus community. Renters can book storage for periods as short as one week and as long as three months; Hosts earn passive income on space they would otherwise leave empty.

At a mid-sized U.S. university with 5,000 international students, each storing an average of $300 worth of goods per summer, the addressable annual gross merchandise value (GMV) exceeds $1.5 million per campus. CampusShare earns a 10% platform commission on completed bookings, yielding an estimated $150,000 in annual revenue per campus at full penetration, with an optional insurance add-on ($5–$15 per booking, tiered by declared item value) providing a secondary revenue stream.

The platform's competitive moat rests on three pillars: the `.edu` identity wall that restricts participation to verified students, a dual-directional review system that builds transaction-level trust over time, and a deposit-escrow mechanism that aligns incentives for both parties. These features create a differentiated value proposition that commercial storage providers cannot replicate and generic marketplace platforms are not structured to offer.
