## Primary Activities (Porter's Value Chain)

| # | Activity | CampusShare Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Inbound Logistics** | Host onboarding: collect unused-space information (dimensions, address, availability window) and verify `.edu` identity to ensure supply-side quality; Renter registration with storage-need declaration |
| 2 | **Operations** | Matching and transaction execution: search ranking, Booking state management, digital agreement generation, deposit escrow, item-photo registration, handover-time coordination |
| 3 | **Outbound Logistics** | Item handover: Renter and Host complete drop-off and return at an agreed on-campus location; the platform generates a timestamped handover certificate |
| 4 | **Marketing & Sales** | Campus-channel marketing: student-organization partnerships, dormitory notice boards, international student office referrals, `.edu` mailing-list outreach; concentrated push in the pre-summer peak window |
| 5 | **Service** | Post-transaction trust maintenance: dual-party reviews, Dispute mediation, customer support, insurance-claim assistance, data retention and deletion compliance |

## Narrative (drop-in paragraph for the report)

CampusShare's primary activities are tightly organized around the Focused Differentiation strategy: Inbound and Marketing use the `.edu` perimeter to keep both sides of the marketplace verified; Operations applies data-driven matching to lower transaction friction; Outbound focuses on walkable, within-campus handovers; and Service accrues platform trust through review and dispute mechanisms.
